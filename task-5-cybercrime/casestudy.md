Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted entity. A common example is a fake email or message that appears to be from a bank or a well-known company.

In a typical phishing attack, the victim receives an email or SMS that looks official and urgent, such as “Your bank account will be blocked. Click here to verify your details.” The message contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website designed to look like the original one. When the user enters their login credentials or financial details, the attacker captures this information.

College students are often targeted because they frequently use online platforms and may not always verify the authenticity of links. The consequences of phishing can be severe, including financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

For example, a student may receive a message claiming to offer a scholarship or internship opportunity and be asked to fill in personal details. Without verifying the source, the student might unknowingly share sensitive information.

This case highlights the importance of being cautious while interacting online and verifying all digital communications before taking action.
